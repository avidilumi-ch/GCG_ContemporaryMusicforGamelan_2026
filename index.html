03
Composers
Arham Aryadi Indonesia

Arham Aryadi is a distinguished Indonesian composer renowned for his innovative approach to music. As the founder and Music Director of the Indonesian Contemporary Gamelan Ensemble (ICGE), he holds a Bachelor's in Music Composition from the Conservatory of Music at Pelita Harapan University and a Master of Arts in Urban Arts Creation from the Postgraduate Jakarta Institute of Arts. He is active in education, teaching at various universities, and co-founded the Indonesian National Orchestra (INO) with composer Franki Raden. Currently Chairman of the Music Committee at the Jakarta Arts Council (2023–2026), his work centres on the fusion of Indonesian traditional music with Western classical and electronic elements. His compositions have been performed by Ensemble Modern, Hong Kong New Music Ensemble, Ensemble Multilatérale, Quatour Bozzini, and others. He is a member of the Asia Traditional Music Committee and serves on the Asian Cultural Council (ACC) Korea.

arhamaryadi.com
Septian Dwi Cahyo Indonesia

Septian Dwi Cahyo is a composer whose work explores the intersection of contemporary music, Indonesian musical practices, and natural pattern systems. He studied composition with Beat Furrer and Sarah Nemtsov at the Kunstuniversität Graz and Mozarteum University Salzburg, developing his practice through Impuls, CIEL Academy, and the Young Composers Meeting. His work has been performed across Southeast Asia, Europe, the United States, East Asia, and Mexico by Talea Ensemble, Orkest de Ereprijs, Spółdzielnia Muzyczna, and Mondrian Ensemble, at festivals including the Andriessen Festival, Limina Festival, and Oranjewoud Festival. He has curated the series Beyond Threshold – New Voices from Indonesia in Bangkok and served as guest editor for Contemporary Music Review Vol. 43, issue 1. His scores are published by BabelScores.

Ivan Liuzzo Italy / Basel New Commission

Ivan Liuzzo is an Italian composer and visual designer of Ethiopian and Eritrean origins, based in Basel. His artistic practice moves between musical composition, visual research, the study of Balinese gamelan, and a deep interest in sacred vocal traditions. His musical work is characterised by instrumental ensembles conceived to allow for long, apparently static stretches of time, in which the non-recognition of the sound source and acoustic hallucination build a state of tension in the listener. In 2025 he received the Global Research Project Grant from Pro Helvetia for a residency in Bali, and a Researchbeitrag from the Canton of Basel-Stadt for Tashtego, a project bringing together voice, gamelan, double bass, and bassoon. His work was published in 2025 by ZOK Records (IREI/Tikur) and Dito Publishing (Gentle Fire Basel, in collaboration with Simone Spampinato).

Werner Kaegi Switzerland · 1926–2024

Werner Kaegi (1926–2024) was a Swiss composer and pioneer of electronic music. A student of Paul Hindemith and Arthur Honegger, he became one of the most influential figures in electroacoustic composition in Europe. From 1971 he worked at the Institute of Sonology in Utrecht, where he developed the VOSIM sound synthesis system and the MIDIM computer composition programme. His work explored the boundaries between acoustic instruments, electronics, and language. Dialogue (3rd Version) (1983–84), for Kendhang solo and electronics, brings his electronic language into dialogue with the percussive tradition of Balinese gamelan. Werner Kaegi passed away in March 2024 at the age of 97.

Dieter Mack Germany

Dieter Mack (b. 1954, Speyer) studied composition, piano and music theory, becoming professor for music theory in Freiburg in 1986 and professor for composition at the MHS Lübeck since 2003. Study and research trips led him mainly to Indonesia (altogether ten years), where he studied Balinese gamelan music; in 1982 he founded his own gamelan ensemble. He worked in Indonesia's music education system from 1988, including as DAAD guest lecturer at UPI Bandung (1992–1995) and as coordinator of a Ford Foundation research project (1996–2007). He gives regular masterclasses in composition across East and Southeast Asia, Canada, and New Zealand, and composes mostly chamber music with a focus on percussion and wind instruments.

dieter-mack.de
Krishna Sutedja Indonesia / Brussels

Krishna Sutedja (b. Jakarta, 1993) is an Indonesian composer, musician, and gamelan specialist based in Brussels. He received his training at the Indonesian Institute of the Arts (ISI) Denpasar and at Codarts University of the Arts in Rotterdam, and is currently active as composer, performer, and gamelan teacher at the Embassy of Indonesia in Brussels. He is the founder and artistic director of Gamelan Mudrasvara, a Brussels-based ensemble dedicated to the performance and promotion of Indonesian music in Europe. He is also a member of the Polyphème ensemble in Paris, where he engages in experimental projects combining gamelan with darbuka and electronic music.

Performers
Wayan Pitriana (Vivi) Bali, Indonesia

Wayan Pitriana (b. Singaraja, Bali, 1988) is an Indonesian singer, musician, and dancer specialising in both traditional and contemporary Balinese performance. Her artistic practice is rooted in Balinese classical traditions while extending into interdisciplinary and international collaborations. She has performed extensively in Indonesia and Europe with Gamelan Mudrasvara, Het Concreet (Tilburg), Dwi Bhumi (Rotterdam), and Nusantara Band (Brussels). Her work integrates vocal, musical, and choreographic expression across traditional repertoire and contemporary stage contexts.

Pietro Elia Barcellona Italy

Pietro Elia Barcellona is an Italian double bassist, composer, and improviser active in contemporary and experimental music. His practice engages both written repertoire and improvisation, exploring their porous boundaries as interdependent processes. He has collaborated with Beat Furrer and Helmut Lachenmann and performed at Manifeste (Paris), Gaudeamus Muziekweek (Utrecht), Musikfestival Bern, Ravenna Festival, and the Holland Festival (Amsterdam). He works regularly with Asko | Schönberg, Collegium Novum Zürich, Ensemble Aventure, Meredith Monk & Vocal Ensemble, and Ensemble Scordatura. He is the recipient of the Kranichsteiner Musikpreis at the Internationale Ferienkurse für Neue Musik Darmstadt (2025), distinctions at the Nicati Competition in Lucerne (Coup de cœur, Honorary Mention) and the John Cage Award in Halberstadt. He holds a Bachelor from the Conservatorium van Amsterdam and a Master in contemporary music performance from the Hochschule für Musik Basel.

Severin Scherrer Basel · b. 1995

Severin Scherrer began his musical education at seven with Swiss percussionist Florian Arbenz. He holds a Bachelor in Music (jazz drums) from the Hochschule Luzern and a Master in Music Pedagogy from the Hochschule der Künste Bern. In 2023 he deepened his knowledge of gamelan music in Bali with one of the island's leading musicians, Pak Cater Ketut, and is a member of the gamelan orchestra Saraswara. He is active in the duo Severin Scherrer & Lennard Fiehn, the trio ORA (odd metres, metric modulations and polyrhythm), and the trio SET (free improvisation). He is a recipient of the Cultural Promotion Prize of the city of Landsberg am Lech.

severinscherrer.com
Gabriella Smart Winterthur · b. 2002

Gabriella Smart began the bassoon at seven and was admitted at thirteen to the Young Students programme at the Zürcher Hochschule der Künste. She later studied with Sergio Azzolini at the Musikhochschule Basel (Bachelor, 2024). A multiple prize-winner at the Swiss Youth Music Competition, she has performed at the Menuhin Festival Gstaad and toured with the Winterthurer Jugendsinfonie Orchester and the Jugendsinfonie Orchester Zürich as principal bassoonist. Recent collaborations include the Chamber Academy Basel and Ensemble Diagonal. She is active in the Neues Orchester Basel, Camerata Cantabile Zurich, and the 21st Century Orchestra Lucerne.

Arthur Wilkens Brazil / Basel · b. 1993

Arthur Wilkens is a composer, keyboard player, and singer specialising in music of the Middle Ages and Renaissance. After studying composition and choral conducting in Brazil, he continued at the Conservatoire Francis Poulenc in Tours and at the Schola Cantorum in Basel, where he obtained a Bachelor and a Master in early keyboards in the class of Corina Marti. He has attended courses in modal singing with Aram Kerovpyan, Marcel Pérès, and Rebecca Stewart. He is currently pursuing artistic research at the Hochschule für Musik Basel, focused on organ improvisation practices at the end of the Middle Ages.

Timm Kornelius Germany / Basel

Timm Kornelius grew up on the Ammersee near Munich and currently pursues a Master's in bassoon with Diego Chenna at the Musik Akademie Basel, following studies with Sergio Azzolini and Karsten Nagel. A scholarship holder of the DAAD and Yehudi Menuhin Live Music Now e.V., in 2021 he founded the auxemble, touring Germany and Georgia. He is a recipient of the Cultural Promotion Prize of the city of Landsberg am Lech and a member of the TONALi-Bühnenakademie. He is active in ensembles spanning experimental, jazz, and pop music, including the Stegreif.Orchester, and engages in electronic music, improvisation, composition, and the study of the baroque bassoon.
